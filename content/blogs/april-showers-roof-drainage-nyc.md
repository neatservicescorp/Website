## The Danger of "Ponding" Water

For the flat roofs common in **Brooklyn and Queens**, drainage is everything. If your roof isn't pitched perfectly toward your drains, water begins to collect in large pools known as "ponding." 

Ponding water is heavy—one inch of water weighs about 5 pounds per square foot. Over time, this weight can cause your roof deck to sag, creating even deeper pools and eventually leading to a structural collapse or major leaks.

## 3 Warning Signs of Poor Drainage

Before the next storm hits, take a look at your exterior and check for these red flags:

1.  **Water Marks on the Fascia:** If you see dark stains on the wood boards behind your gutters, water is likely backing up and seeping behind the siding.
2.  **Foundation Erosion:** Check the ground where your downspouts exit. If you see deep holes in the dirt or cracks in the pavement, your system isn't directing water far enough away from your foundation.
3.  **The "Waterfall" Effect:** During a rainstorm, if water is pouring over the *sides* of your gutters instead of through the downspouts, you have a major clog that needs immediate attention.

## Why Gutter Maintenance is Non-Negotiable

Gutters and scuppers are your roof’s first line of defense. A simple **NYC gutter cleaning** in early April can save you from a **$10,000 roof repair** in May. We recommend ensuring that all downspouts have extensions to carry water at least 6 feet away from the building’s base.
