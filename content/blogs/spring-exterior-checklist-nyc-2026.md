Spring has officially arrived in New York. While it’s the perfect time to enjoy the warmer weather, it is also the most critical time to assess the toll winter took on your property. Freezing temperatures, ice storms, and rapid thaws can leave behind hidden structural damages that, if ignored, turn into expensive summer repairs.

As a trusted **general contractor in New York**, Neat Services Corp has put together the ultimate 2026 checklist for **spring home maintenance in NYC**. Here is everything you need to inspect right now:

## 1. Roof Inspection & Insurance Claim Review
Start at the top. Look for missing, cracked, or curling shingles caused by winter winds and ice dams. If you suspect severe damage, now is the time to review your homeowner's insurance policy. A professional inspection can help document storm damage before the deadline for filing a winter-related claim expires.

## 2. Gutter Cleaning and Drainage
Winter debris, pine needles, and shingle granules often clog gutters by late March. 
* **Clear the blockages:** Ensure water can flow freely to prevent fascia board rot.
* **Check the downspouts:** Make sure water is being directed at least 5 to 10 feet away from your home's foundation to prevent basement flooding during April showers.

## 3. Siding and Masonry Cracks
The freeze-thaw cycle causes building materials to expand and contract.
* **Siding:** Inspect your vinyl, wood, or fiber cement siding for cracks, warping, or pieces that have pulled away from the house.
* **Masonry:** Check brick facades, chimneys, and retaining walls for crumbling mortar (spalling) or deep cracks. Sealing these now prevents water infiltration.

## 4. Window Seal Failure
Take a close look at your exterior windows. If you notice condensation trapped *between* the panes of glass, the weather seal has failed. This compromises your home's energy efficiency, meaning higher AC bills this summer. Also, check and replace any cracked caulking around the window frames.

## 5. Deck Safety and Wood Rot
Before you bring out the patio furniture, give your deck a thorough safety check. Look for soft spots in the wood, loose railings, and popped nails. Moisture from sitting snow can cause rapid wood rot, making elevated decks hazardous.
