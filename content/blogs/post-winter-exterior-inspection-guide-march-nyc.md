As the winter days shorten and the first signs of milder weather arrive in New York, the first week of March presents us with a golden opportunity. It’s the perfect time to make a smart transition: from repairing winter emergencies to proactively preparing our property for the spring to come.

At **Neat Services Corp**, we know that the freeze-thaw cycle of the New York winter is brutal on exterior materials. An early inspection this week can save you thousands of dollars and many headaches when the April rains arrive.

Here are the 5 key points you need to inspect on your home's exterior right now:

## 1. Complete Roof Inspection
Wind, snow, and ice can have weakened the structure.
* **What to look for:** Missing, cracked, or curling shingles. Pay close attention to the flashing around chimneys and skylights, as these are common points of leakage after winter. An **NYC roof inspection** now will give you peace of mind.

## 2. Clean and Check Gutters and Downspouts
During the fall and winter, gutters accumulate leaves and debris that freeze over.
* **What to look for:** Blockages preventing water flow. Ensure downspouts direct water at least 5 to 10 feet away from your home's foundation to prevent basement flooding and foundation damage. (*Brooklyn gutter cleaning*).

## 3. Siding and Foundation
Temperature fluctuations can cause cracks in siding or concrete.
* **What to look for:** Signs of water damage, mold, or areas where the siding is separating from the house. Check visible foundations for new cracks that should be sealed. (*Queens siding repair*).

## 4. Window and Door Seals
Extreme cold and wind can deteriorate caulking and weatherstripping.
* **What to look for:** Cracks or gaps around frames. If you notice condensation between panes of glass, the seal has failed. Replacing it now will improve your energy efficiency for the summer. (*New York window replacement*).

## 5. General Yard Cleanup and Preparation
Remove fallen branches, accumulated leaves, and any winter debris.
* **What to do:** Clean walkways and decks to prevent slipping on residual mold. This is the perfect time to check and prepare your lawnmower and other tools for their first use. (*NYC spring yard cleanup*).
